ABOUT
Chezy Champs is an annual off-season FIRST robotics tournament hosted by Team 254. The tournament takes place in late September, at Bellarmine College Preparatory in San Jose, California. This fall, Team 254 is hosting the second annual Chezy Champs, a premiere off-season FIRST Robotics competition event. 38 teams will come together in San Jose to battle it out to determine the best in the west.

INFORMATION
Chezy Champs features an entertaining show, competent volunteers, and a focus on the team experience. There will also be 254-style photo shoots for all robots Friday evening, Food trucks, Blue banners and awesome trophies, and rule changes that go further than IRI in fixing Recycle Rush.

When: Saturday and Sunday, September 19-20, 2015

Where: Bellarmine College Preparatory, San Jose, California

Registration: $300 ($600 for field-side pit)

Capacity: 38 teams

RULE CHANGES

Chezy Champs will feature 13 rule changes that go farther than IRI in fixing Recycle Rush.

1. Each alliance may place up to three additional recycling containers anywhere on their side of the field, except for the AUTO ZONE, prior to the match.

2. The left-hand (when viewed from the alliance station) line of five gray totes against the step will be right-side up.

3. The right-hand (when viewed from the alliance station) line of five gray totes against the step will be removed and the totes will be placed in the alliance station instead. The space they occupied will be filled by moving the remaining nine right-hand landfill totes up against the step, while maintaining their orientation relative to each other.

4. During the autonomous period, robots may not contact the two recycling containers on the left side of the step (when viewed from the alliancestation). Violation: Red card.

5. No points are awarded for processed or unprocessed litter, but litter in recycling containers will be scored as per the official rules.

6. One alliance's litter may not contact anything on the other alliance's side of the field. Violation: Yellow card.

7. Litter may be introduced over the alliance station wall at any point during the teleoperated period.

8. Causing yellow totes to transfer to the other side of the field while attempting to coopertate will not result in a foul.

9. Yellow totes are counted as gray totes when scored on the scoring platform, during the entire tournament.

10. Playoff rounds are head-to-head, best of three, using the 2014 bracket structure.

11. Any number of alliance members may be within the human player zone during the teleoperated period.

12. Alliance members may contact the chute door and totes simultaneously.

13. Teams must be able to demonstrate, if asked by the head referee, that their robot fits within the TRANSPORT CONFIGURATION, but may transport their robot however they see fit.
